Season's Greetings!


 

LaTour Friends,

I hope the end of the year finds all of you wiser and anticipatory for the coming year. If asked how I felt the LaTour Community faired this year, I would have to say, “We held our own!” As much as 2020 was unpredictable, 2021 was revealing and centering. As a community LaTour has pressed through the fears that covid brought, pressed into helping each other through difficulties, and pressed on to new life and perspectives. 

On the other side of a major world event that has shifted the economy, government, and what we viewed as normal processes, the LaTour community is facing the need to make adjustments as well. As we face higher costs for services and products, shifts will be needed in the means to provide for the resources valued by LaTour. Everyone has embraced the changes brought on and shown patience and grace while walking through the challenging last 2 years. Together, we will make the shifts needed to see the LaTour community into this new season.

I am so happy we are once again able to gather for celebrations, parties, and fun! I pray for each of you to be filled with faith and hope. May you have a wonderful holiday season, Merry Christmas, and Happy New Year!
Terri Powell
President

LaTour Updates
 
Building Structural Assessment
In October, EFI Global did a walk through of the building and will be providing a report on any structural assessment considerations for the building. They had no immediate concerns.
 
Boiler & Chiller Evaluation
A thorough evaluation is being done on the boiler/chiller systems to set up maintenance processes to help stabilize utility usage and repairs to equipment.
 
Wi-Fi Improvement
A tip for better wifi in units is to add a wifi extender to your existing router. Terri recently did this and found it to work and relieve much frustration at a low cost!
 
Pigeon Deterrent Update
We're continuing to explore ways to eliminate the pigeon issue. A new possibility is a product that repels through scent, texture, and sight. Be watching for more information on this coming soon!

In Memoriam
 

MJ Nelson
October 24, 1937 - March 9, 2021
 
MJ devoted her life to community involvement, volunteerism, and leadership in the Amarillo area from the classroom, the arts, and her faith. She was known for her big heart and gentle nature. 
 
She is survived by her daughter Meg and husband Bill, son Rod and wife Missy, and grandson Brady.

Diane Baker
September 30, 1932 - May 22, 2021
 
Diane had a very distinguished career in marketing and tourism, having established both Santa Fe, New Mexico, and Huntington Beach, California into popular travel destinations during the course of her life. She will be remembered for her energy, compassion, knowledge, savviness, generosity, and tremendous love of her family and friends.

She is survived by her three daughters, Cheri and husband Jim, Pamela and husband Charley, and Karen, and husband Four. She is survived by four grandchildren, Brooke and husband Fisher, Cody and fiancée Madison, Abigail, and Thomas.

Teenye Price
December 27, 1931 - August 23, 2021
 
Teenye was a loving mother & wife, an active member of community groups, and a faithful servant to God. She will be remembered as a sweet soul that was dearly loved by those closest to her.

She is survived by her husband, Jim; daughter, Jamie and husband Bobby; son, Jimmy, and wife Dulcie; grandson, Rob, and wife Farrar; and two great-granddaughters.

Melba Oakes
November 23, 1937 - October 3, 2021

Melba was a devoted wife and mother who loved painting, crocheting, and cooking. She was known for her kind and hospitable nature and love of family and friends.

She is survived by her husband, Jack; her children, Stephanie and Husband, Luis, Jan and Husband, Mark, Pam, and Teresa; her five grandchildren, Lacee and Husband, Derrel, Brady, Aaron, Matthew, and Audrey and Husband, Jim; and 7 great grandchildren.

Ed Fancher
December 22, 1926 - September 19, 2021

Ed was a well-respected member of the community from his work in the insurance business, his philanthropic efforts, and his devotion to his friends and family. He will be remembered for his leadership, sense of humor, and helpful nature.
 
Ed is survived by his wife, Shirley, and his children, Edward and wife Katherine, and Ann; grandchildren, Taylor, Edward, Helen, and Jim; his brother, Robert, and wife, Karin; nephews Mark and wife Rhonda, John and wife Diana; and grandnieces, Haley, Allison, and Kate.
